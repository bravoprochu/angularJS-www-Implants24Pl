METAL BACK

For over 20 years, FH ORT HOPEDICS has specialized in the production of cementless total hip prostheses. The ATLAS acetabular cup was developed using this expertise and the latest technological advances in terms of bearing, metallurgy and instrumentation.

THE CONCEPT

The ATLAS concept, born in 1985 as a result of Dr Alain Dambreville’s research. This cementless elastic acetabular cup immediately appealed to a large number of surgeons, who continue to implant several thousand each year, across the world. This is success is due in part to its easiness to implant and its excellent results, linked to its essential principles:
1- Primary stability, through its press-fit;
2- Stability of the insert inside the metal-back;
3- Secondary fixation due to surface coatings;
4- Thickness of the PE.

RANGE AND MATERIALS

The three types of ATLAS metal-back are made from a titanium alloy, TA 6V4 ELI, with a constant 2.5 mm thickness. All three include:
- a slot providing the cup with the elasticity to ensure a perfect and instant press-fit and risk-free impacting for the supporting bone.
- inside sand–blasted and a cylindrical equatorial zone, to ensure the insert’s stability.
- a hydroxyapatite coating for secondary fixation.

The ATLAS IIIP is fitted with 4 spikes which fit into the bone to avoid any rotation of the cup and also screw holes for screw fixation, if necessary.

The ATLAS IVP also has spikes, but no screw holes.

The ATLAS MS was designed without spikes, to meet LIS requirements (Less Invasive Surgery). Like the ATLAS III P, it has 3 screw holes. The four spikes are replaced with a rougher surface coating, obtained through projection of T40 porous titanium, before the HAP.

The ATLAS metal-back range can be combined with a wide range of highly cross-linked polyethylene (TRIANON) or conventional inserts, available in various internal diameters (22.22; 28; 32 and 36mm) and shapes (flat-edged; posterior wall; anti-dislocation wall).
