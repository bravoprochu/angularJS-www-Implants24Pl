DELTOPECTORAL APPROACH

The cutaneous incision starts from the clavicle and follows the deltopectoral groove vertically, level with the coracoid process (in green).

Once the cutaneous incision has been made, the groove is located by the cephalic vein, which should be left on the deltoid side.

After locating the lateral border of the short head of the biceps, the axillary clavipectoral fascia is incised and the subscapularis exposed. The axillary nerve is located with a finger.

Retractors are inserted to retract:
1. the deltoid laterally
2. the short head of the biceps medially.

When the sheath of the biceps on the anterior surface of the metaphysis of the humerus is located, the long head of the biceps is sometimes found to be pathological and a tenodesis may be necessary if the tendon is damaged.

By following the long head of the biceps the rotator interval can be found and opened and the superior border of the subscapularis

After ligating the anterior circumflex vessels, the subscapularis tendon is sectioned and marked with threads.

If necessary, the superior border of the pectoralis major tendon can be incised for 2 to 3 cm.
