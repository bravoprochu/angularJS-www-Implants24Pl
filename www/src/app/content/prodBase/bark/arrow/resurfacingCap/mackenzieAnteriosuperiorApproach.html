MACKENZIE ANTERIOSUPERIOR APPROACH This approach is more lateral than the deltopectoral approach (in red).

The cutaneous incision begins perpendicular to the acromioclavicular joint, extends towards the anterior angle of the acromion and extends laterally and inferiorly for 5 cm.

The superficial surface of the deltoid is exposed. At the anterolateral angle of the acromion, there is usually a raphe marking the line between the middle and anterior bundles of the deltoid. This fascia is divided in the direction of the muscle fibres. The inferior border of the subacromial-subdeltoid bursa in general marks the limit which should not be exceeded if any risk of damaging the terminal part of the axillary nerve is to be avoided.

The anterior bundle of the deltoid may be released subperiosteally from the anterior border of the acromion to the anterior part of the acromioclavicular joint, or even a little further medially towards the clavicle. Be sure to maintain the continuity of the deltotrapezial fascia. Similarly, the middle bundle of the deltoid may be released for a few millimetres subperiosteally from the anterolateral border of the acromion.

The coracoacromial ligament is detached from the acromial mass and an acromioplasty can be performed if necessary. The anterior superior part of the head of the humerus is thus exposed. The rotator interval must be located, and is more easily identifiable internally, at the foot of the coracoid.

The rotator interval is opened outwards, along the superior border of the tendon of the subscapularis muscle as far as the point where the tendon of the long head of the biceps leaves the bicipital groove.

The tendon of the subscapularis muscle will be cut with the anterior capsule, 1 cm medial to its insertion on the lesser tuberosity as far as the anterior circumflex pedicle.

After dislocation of the head of the humerus, with the arm in external rotation and slight abduction, it must be completely exposed, so that the entire circumference of the anatomical neck can be seen, including posteriorly. The size of the implant to be fitted can be chosen using the humeral head template. It is essential to position it above and against the insertion of the supraspinatus, 1 cm anterior to the insertion of the infraspinatus.

Take into account particularly the anteroposterior diameter; remove large osteophytes as you externally rotate using a medium osteotome.

The two directional guide rods (inclination: 135°/retroversion: 20°) are reference indicators for angles to adjust and position the implant.

Inclination rod:
To be aligned with the humerus. The vertical corresponds to a cervico-diaphyseal angle of 135°.

Retroversion rod:
Aligned with the forearm, it corresponds to retroversion of the head of the humerus of 20° (the retroversion can be greater than 20°).

The periphery of the template must perfectly match the anatomical neck.
