COMMON RULES
1.1 Positionning of the patient

The patient is in a semi-seated position. The affected shoulder should extend clearly beyond the edge of the operating table. The forearm should be resting on an armrest.
The arm is in 25-30° retropulsion in relation to the thorax to liberate the point of entry.
The fluoroscope should be placed longitudinally at the level of the patient’s head (fig. 1).

1.2 Point of entry

The point of entry is found at the articular summit of the head of the humerus, in front of the acromion (fig. 2).
The awl square trepanates the head of the humerus (fluoroscope).
A proximal reamer measuring 7, 8 or 9 mm (depending on the diameter of the nail chosen) calibrates the entry opening (fig. 3). The nail and its holder (ref. 253 671 and ref. 253 680) are then introduced (fig. 4).

1.3 Length of the nail

The proximal end must imperatively be below the bony articular level of the head of the humerus (fig. 5).
Marking with the fluoroscope is necessary.
