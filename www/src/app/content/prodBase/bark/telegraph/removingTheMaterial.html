4. REMOVING THE MATERIAL

The TELEGRAPH ® should not normally be removed in elderly patients. Two situations can nevertheless result in its removal being necessary:

>> 1. The screws and/or nail are protruding and causing discomfort.
In this situation, removal will be easy using material that is easy to find.
The installation is still the same (§1.1) with the fluoroscope to do the removal percutaneously.

>> 2. The patient is young.
Removal may be difficult if the material is deeply embedded. The installation is the same.
The proximal extremity must be identified: using a metal pin with the fluoroscope makes identification possible. The nail-holder is then positioned on the nail. The guides make it possible to find the screws (fig. 22).

The nail holder is used to find the position of the screws. It should not be used to remove the nail. The nail must be removed with an extractor masselotte with screws on to the M5 connective screw. The masselotte and its axis are optional. If you plan to do removals, you should consider ordering them.
