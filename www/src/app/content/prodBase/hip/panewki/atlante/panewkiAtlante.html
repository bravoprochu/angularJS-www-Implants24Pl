Press-fit cementless cup

Cementless press-fit cup  The ATLANTE 2 cup has been specially designed to provide a perfectly suitable interface for the bone elasticity module, but also allowing easy, precise and safe implementation of a ceramic insert.

The pre-mounted ring around the ceramic is designed to provide maximum protection to the ceramic, in cases of extreme positions, whilst maintaining the range of motion and stability associated with largediameter heads.

Depending on the user's preference, the ATLANTE 2 cup can be used with either a ceramic insert or a TRIANON® highly cross-linked polyethylene insert, in the same metal-back.

Press-fit cup

For over 20 years, FH ORTHOPEDICS has been specialising in the production of cementless implants for total hip prosthesis. The ATLANTE II cup applies the latest technological progress in terms of bearings, metallurgy and instrumentation to this expertise.

Concept

The concept of the ATLANTE 2 cup is based on our experience with the metal-back ATLANTE cup, in use since 2001; combined with current bearings.

The ATLANTE 2 cup provides surgeons with a choice, based on the indications and patient age, between a TRIANON® highly crosslinked PE liner or a massive ceramic insert with a titanium ring, with a large-diameter head.

Both types of inserts allow large diameter heads to be used, to avoid problems of instability and wear.

Range and materials

The ATLANTE 2 range of rigid cementless cups includes 10 sizes with diameters ranging from 46mm to 64mm in 2mm increments.

The metal-back cups are made from a Ti Al6 V4 titanium alloy. The external surface is sand-blasted and has a hydroxyapatite coating, which promotes secondary fixation of the implant.

The inserts are made from:

  • TRIANON® highly crosslinked PE ;
  • Biolox® Delta® ceramic for its recognised tribological properties and its increased fracture resistance, with a titanium ring.

Metal-back Press-fit

The ATLANTE 2 rigid cup metal-back is a hemispheric cup with an equatorial flare, due to its serrated macrostructure, which give it an excellent press-fit and perfect primary fixation.

Metal-back

The internal surface of the metal-back has been designed to provide perfect fixation of inserts. Ceramic inserts are fixed by wedging, using a micro-threaded 14° morse taper. PE inserts are fixed using 12 notches, mass-produced, which hold the slots in the polyethylene, to direct it in 30° steps. It also has a lip for increased stability. The cup has three holes for the use of flat-head titanium cancellous bone screws, if necessary, which are available in sizes 15 to 40mm (a screw rack is included in the instrumentation set).

PE liner

The polyethylene liners for the ATLANTE 2 cup are highly crosslinked (TRIANON®). This material improves the bearing to reduce wear, but also allows the use of large-diameter heads (32mm and 36mm).

The TRIANON® polyethylene inserts have a minimum thickness of 8mm, to avoid any risk of creepage. They are fitted with 12 notches to prevent movement of the liner and allow the exterior wall to be aligned. The lip is present to fix inserts by clipping into the cup. These liners are available in 28mm, 32mm and 36mm diameters, in a flat-edged version (charnley type), or with a 4 mm external wall (called STANDARD PE).

Cera mic Insert

The massive ceramic inserts for the ATLANTE 2 cup are made from Biolox® Delta® alumina, fitted with a factory-set titanium ring. This allows the use of Ø32mm and Ø36mm heads, thus reducing the risk of luxation. This type of ceramic is recognised for its fracture-resistance properties, but also for its hardness, reducing wear on the insert and ensuring its longevity.

The setting of the titanium ring, with its internal groove, ensures easy fitting using its gripper, and allows ideal positioning of the implant. The titanium ring also facilitates extraction, if necessary.

In the event of impingement, the ring will be the first element in contact with the neck, protecting the ceramic from any conflict.
