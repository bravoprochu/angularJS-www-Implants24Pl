Cemented femoral stem

The cemented Thira® stem is made of stainless steel 316L ; the surface is highly polished as ratest cementing techniques require.

The shape is selflocking and has a tapered shape allowing for optimal anteroposteral filling. The cervico diaphyseal angle is 130°. It exists in 9 different sizes, from 2 to 10 (size 1 is available on special request). The necks are of increasing length for efficient adaptation to the patients’ anatomy.

The cemented Thira® stem has the same geometry as the cementless Thira® stem, the arthroplasty can therefore be performed with the same instrumentation, whether the stem is cemented or not.

  • Cervico diasphyseal angle of 130°.
  • 12/14 taper allowing an alumina/alumina couple to be used
  • Diameter of neck : 12.5, in order to avoid impingements

Uncemented femoral stem

More than10 years of clinical monitoring has validated the biomechanical principles which characterize the uncemented femoral stem with trabecular grooves : THIRA.

Basic principles of the THIRA stem :

  • Primary fixation on the three-dimensional blocking principle.
  • Improvement of area in contact with the metaphysial region (grooves cut in quarter-circle improve penetration into metaphysial cancellous bone).
  • Optimization of secondary fastening by spraying HAP over 2/3 of its surface.
  • Extractability without femoral flap (the distal end of the THIRA stem is mirror-polished and anodized).
  • Provides an alternative to fitting an uncemented THIRA stem in the event of a weak bony support, by installing a cemented version with identical design (Quartos T stem. See specific documention).
  • Cervico-diaphysial angle of 130°.
  • 12/14 taper for using an alumina/alumina pair.
  • Neck diameter 12,5 mm to reduce the risk of impingement.
